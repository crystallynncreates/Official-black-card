Homeschooling

Know your rights, know the law, know what your school district owes your family — plus free resources, extracurriculars, and grants for K-12 kids.

Not legal advice.

Homeschool law changes and varies by state. We only state a specific legal requirement when we're confident it's accurate and current — everywhere else, we link you straight to HSLDA and your state Department of Education so you get it from the source, not a guess.

Your rights

What your school district owes homeschoolers

In most states, homeschool families have the right to: request textbooks/materials the district must provide free of charge in some states, dual-enroll in community college courses, and access special-education evaluation services through the district if needed. Exact rules are set state-by-state and sometimes district-by-district.
